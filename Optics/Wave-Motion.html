What is a particle?

Normally, we imagine that a particle is basically something like a ball - which we shrink down to the smallest size possible. We imagine that the ball is localized - what this means is that the ball “interacts” with other objects around it on a short scale of distance (normally envisioned to be the actual physical size of the ball!). But the ball also has a gravitational field, and it does interact with things that are incredibly far away from it. This interaction is of course very weak, but it is present nonetheless!

This means that a particle is not strictly “localized” - essentially a particle a wave and a wave is a particle. We need to specify our scales to be sure of which one we want to deal with in any given problem.

What is a wave?

Classical wave

A classical travelling wave is self-sustaining disturbance of a medium, which moves through space transporting energy and momentum.

We normally think of a wave as an entity that is continuous and exists over an extended region. But looking closely at a wave, we can notice that it is formed of a vast number of particles that are moving in some concerted way. Several waves that we see are formed on a particulate media.

But, electromagnetic waves (and as a more exotic example, Gravitational waves) do not propagate through particulate media. Thus, on a macroscopic level, EM Waves serve as the ideal example (and model) of a wave that is entirely detached from particulate media. But on the microscopic (i.e. quantum) level, the electromagnetic wave is essentially a statistical manifestation of a more “granular” phenomena - it is not really continuous.

One-Dimensional Waves

There are two major kinds of physical waves that we can observe around us:

  • Longitudinal: The medium is displaced in the direction of the motion of the wave. Ex: Sound Waves.
  • Transverse: The medium is displaced in a direction perpendicular to that of the motion of the wave. In both these case, though, the disturbance advances and not the medium. It is precisely this property of the wave that allows it to travel at great speeds!
