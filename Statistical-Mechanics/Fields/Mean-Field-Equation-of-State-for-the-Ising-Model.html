The Mean-Field Approximation

To solve this problem, we first attempt to simplify it slightly. We replaced all of the detailed, fluctuating interactions that any single spin feels from its neighbours with a single, average or mean field.

When is such an approximation justified?

While the precise demarcation of domains in which mean-field approximation works requires some involved discussion, we can roughly say this - The approximation is justifiable in systems where each spin interacts with a very large number of other spins (this happens in lattices where there are more “vertices” or on higher dimensions), as the law of large numbers suggests that the fluctuations around the average will be small.
