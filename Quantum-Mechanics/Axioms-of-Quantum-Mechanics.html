Axioms of Quantum Mechanics

Axioms of Quantum Mechanics

Dec 03, 20252 min read

  • Quantum
  • QM
  • Axioms

The following are the axioms of Quantum Mechanics -

  1. The properties of quantum systems are completely defined by the specification of its state vector, denoted generally by ∣ψ⟩. The state vector is an element of a complex Hilbert Space H, called the space of states.

  2. With every physical property A, there exists a corresponding linear, Hermitian operator A called an observable, which acts in the Hilbert space H.

    • The eigenvalues of these Hermitian operators are values of the physical properties.

  3. (Born Rule) If ∣ψ⟩ is the vector in H representing the state of the system, and ∣ϕ⟩ represents a particular physical state of the system, we can define a probability P(∣ψ⟩,∣ϕ⟩) for finding ∣ψ⟩ in the state ∣ϕ⟩. This is given by,

P(∣ψ⟩,∣ϕ⟩)=∣⟨ψ∣ϕ⟩∣2

Where ⟨⋅∣⋅⟩ denotes the inner product defined on the Hilbert Space.

  1. (Wave function collapse) If A is an observable with the eigenvalues listed in the set {an​} and the eigenvectors listed in the set {∣n⟩}, and we have a system in a given state ∣ψ⟩, the probability of obtaining an​ as the “outcome” of the measurement of A (the physical property corresponding to the observable), i.e. on the action of A on ∣ψ⟩ is given by,
P(an​)=∣⟨n∣ψ⟩∣2

After such a measurement operation is done, the system is left in the state ∣n⟩, i.e the wave function ∣ψ⟩ “collapses” to that state.

  1. The time-evolution of a closed system as described in Quantum Mechanics is unitary, i.e. reversible. The evolution is given by the time-dependent Schrodinger equation -
iℏ∂t∂∣ψ⟩​=H∣ψ⟩

Where H is the Hamiltonian of the system, ℏ is the reduced Planck constant, and i is −1​.
