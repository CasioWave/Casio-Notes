TL;DR

Stimulated Emission is an interesting phenomena, where a system decays from an energetic state to a less energetic state due to an external photon coupling to it. The crucial phenomena is that the photon radiated from the system due to this event is identical to the original external photon in all ways - it has the same polarization, phase, direction of propagation, etc.

Einstein coefficients

Note that the entire calculation done above was based on a coherent driving electromagnetic wave. If we were to do this for thermal radiation fields interacting with the system, we would need to integrate with the spectral energy density of the radiation field instead. Following through with this will lead to an expression for the Einstein coefficients, as they are described in Spontaneous Emission. This derivation is skipped here, for now.

It might also be of interest to note that Spontaneous Emission still occurs when , i.e. in vacuum. In a fully quantum mechanical treatment, where the electromagnetic field is also considered to be quantized, the vacuum fluctuation contribution keeps the emission transition rate non-zero even when there are no photons present.
